POINT

Features of Temple Yoga

Set within the sacred grounds of a centuries-old temple, this yoga retreat offers
a rare opportunity to attune the senses and find harmony with nature.
Immersed in spiritual heritage, guests are invited to slow down, be present,
and rediscover their inner self.

  • A temple steeped in history and tradition offers a serene, sacred atmosphere where stillness and purity abound. Practicing yoga in this tranquil setting awakens the senses and brings deep balance to both body and mind. Step away from the noise of everyday life and take time to reconnect with yourself—gently, mindfully, and with intention.

  • Start your day with clarity and intention through morning rituals unique to temple life. Experience workshops only a temple can offer— such as sutra copying and meditation—deeply rooted in spiritual discipline, designed to calm the mind and sharpen your focus.

  • Shojin cuisine is more than just a meal— it's a mindful practice that nourishes both body and spirit. Made entirely from plant-based ingredients such as seasonal vegetables, legumes, and grains, this traditional Buddhist cuisine avoids all animal products. Through simple yet thoughtful preparation methods that highlight the natural flavors of each ingredient, Shojin cuisine offers a low- calorie, nutrient-rich dining experience that honors balance, seasonality, and purity.

SERVICE

OTERA STAY Service

  • OTERA STAY partners with over 60 temples across Japan, including some located along the sacred UNESCO World Heritage site of the Kumano Kodo pilgrimage route. We offer personalized recommendations based on your preferred travel time, group size, and specific needs.

  • To ensure a smooth and comfortable experience, our facilities are equipped with essential yoga props and reliable internet connectivity.

    In addition to individual guests, we also welcome group and corporate yoga retreats.

  • Our clean, well-maintained rooms are based on traditional Japanese-style interiors and come equipped with modern heating and air conditioning.Surrounded by the calming scent of tatami mats, you'll find yourself naturally taking deep breaths and settling into a state of peace. Enjoy your stay in a space infused with the quiet elegance of Japanese aesthetics.

  • We serve Shojin cuisine, traditional plant-based temple cuisine made with locally sourced vegetables and tofu. Depending on the temple, other meal options may also be available.

    Upon request, a selection of alcoholic beverages such as beer and wine can be provided to complement your dining experience.

  • In addition to traditional experiences such as meditation, sutra copying, and tea ceremony, we also offer a variety of activities including sauna sessions and outdoor adventures for a well-rounded and rejuvenating stay.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Wi-Fi available?
Yes. Wi-Fi is available in most temple lodgings' main halls, accommodation areas, and common rooms.
Can we drink alcohol?
Yes, alcohol is permitted at all temple lodgings.
Some locations may restrict where drinking is allowed.
Please let us know in advance if you'd like alcohol with meals. For after-parties, you're welcome to bring your own.
What equipment is available?
We provide whiteboards, screens, projectors, cushions, chairs, and tables.
*Available equipment varies by location. Please inquire for details.
Can you arrange meals?
Yes, all locations can arrange meals. Please let us know which meals you need for each day.
*Some locations may only offer boxed meals.
■ Do you provide yoga mats and yoga blocks?
Yes, we do.
■ What is the minimum number of participants required?
Also, what is the maximum group size you can accommodate?
It depends on the temple, but generally from 2 people. The maximum group size we can accommodate is up to 100 people, depending on the temple.
Is there air conditioning and heating?
All guest rooms are fully equipped. Some main halls and common areas may not have these facilities.
■ Can I change the check-in and check-out times?
Yes, that's possible.
Please let us know your preferred time. Late checkout on departure day is also available.
*Times may need to be adjusted based on other bookings.
Is there a curfew?
Generally no.
However, if you want meals, please arrive during our service hours.
■ Can I store my luggage before check-in or after check-out?
Yes, you can.
■ Do you only accept cash?
It depends on the temple, but many accept credit cards.
■ If credit cards are accepted, which ones can I use?
VISA, Mastercard, JCB, American Express, Diners Club are generally accepted.
■ Is parking available? If so, is there a fee?
It depends on the temple. Please inquire for details.
■ How do you handle the storage of valuables?
It depends on the temple. Please inquire for details.
